Careers

Role:

A forestry manager oversees the sustainable management and conservation of forests, ensuring that logging and resource extraction do not harm the ecosystem. They implement forest conservation plans, manage forest fire prevention, and work to balance resource extraction with ecological health.

Skills Needed:

  • Knowledge of ecology, forestry techniques, environmental regulations, and conservation principles. Good project management and organizational skills are also valuable.

Work Environments:

  • Forestry managers work for government agencies, environmental organizations, and private forestry companies.

Role:

Environmental consultants in forestry provide expert advice on forest management and conservation to businesses, NGOs, and government bodies. They help organizations comply with environmental laws and implement sustainable practices, such as reforestation or reduced-impact logging.

Skills Needed:

  • Background in environmental science, ecology, or forestry. Strong analytical, communication, and problem-solving skills are essential, as consultants often work with diverse stakeholders.

Work Environments:

  • Environmental consulting firms, research institutions, or as independent consultants.
